Features of the Railway Parcel Tracking System

The Railway Parcel Tracking System is designed to streamline and enhance the management of parcels transported through the railway network. This system allows for seamless tracking, secure handling, and efficient management of parcels across multiple stations, ensuring that both staff and administrators can manage the parcel flow effectively.

Real-Time Parcel Tracking

Track parcels in real time as they move through various railway stations, with instant updates on the current location and status.

Role-Based Access

Provides separate dashboards for Admins and Staff. Admins can manage users, while Staff can update parcel information and statuses.

Parcel History and Status Updates

View the complete history of each parcel, including time-stamped status updates at every station it passes through.

Station-Based Parcel Transactions

Manage parcel send and receive transactions at each station, ensuring smooth and secure parcel handling across locations.

Sri Lankan Railway Parcel Transport System

The Sri Lankan Railway Parcel Transport System is a cornerstone for both small and medium enterprises and individual customers, helping them connect, trade, and thrive across the island.

Sri Lanka Railways ( SLR ) is a government department functioning under the Ministry of Transport. It is a major transport service provider and is the only rail transport organization in the country. SLR transports both passenger and freight. At its inception, railway was carrying more freight than passenger. But today, it is passenger oriented. SLRâ€™s market share for passenger transport is about 6.0 % and about 0.7 % for goods transport. In Sri Lanka, the service provided by SLR in carrying the daily commuters to their workplaces, is inevitable. Sri Lanka Railway operates approximately 396 trains which include 67 Long-Distance and 16 Intercity trains and carries about 3.72 Million passengers daily. SLR owns and maintains 1561km of rail tracks, 72 locomotives,power sets 78, 565 carriages and the signalling network. At present, it has a workforce of 17,634. Sri Lanka Railways ( SLR ) functions under the General Manager of Railways (GMR). The General Manager reports to the Secretary of the Ministry of Transport. SLR has been divided into ten Sub Departments and three Units. Sub departments are managed by the Heads of the Sub Departments who reports directly to the General Manager of Railways.
