Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

You can send a variety of items, including packages, documents, equipment, and bulk goods. However, items that are perishable, hazardous, or restricted by law may not be allowed. Always check with your local railway station for specific item restrictions.

Parcel bookings can be made at designated railway stations. You will need to bring your parcel to the station, where it will be weighed, labeled, and assigned a tracking number.

Yes, there are size and weight limits for parcels. These limits may vary depending on the train and available cargo space. It’s recommended to check with the railway station beforehand to confirm the maximum allowable weight and dimensions for your shipment.

Yes, Sri Lankan Railways offers tracking services for parcels. Once your parcel is registered and assigned a tracking number, you can use this number to check its status and ensure it reaches its destination on time.

Delivery times vary based on the destination and train schedules. Generally, deliveries within the same region may be faster, while cross-country deliveries might take longer. It’s best to check the estimated delivery times at the time of booking.

If there is a delay or if you cannot locate your parcel, contact the railway station where you booked the service. They can assist you with tracking updates or help resolve any issues with your shipment.

Payments can typically be made at the booking counter, either by cash or card. Some stations may also offer online or mobile payment options.

Service availability may vary by station, but many stations operate on weekends and select public holidays. It’s best to confirm the operating hours with your nearest railway station.
